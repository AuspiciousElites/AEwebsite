Our Events

Auspicious Elites Visits October 201Boys2 Men Leadership Talk - October 2015

The aim of this programme is to expose young men to positive role models and to help them make informed choices about their future. The event was held at uMdlamfe High School and involved both Grade 11 and 12 male learners.

Our guest speaker was ABI’s Marketing Coordinator, Mr Sifiso Manyana, who gave an inspirational lecture to his young audience, which covered issues of time management, respect of authority, choosing the right friends and also highlighting the role of a man in society. Ms Thembile Jobe, CSI manager at Foskor represented the sponsors of the event highlighted the importance of education and choosing careers in scarce skills sectors. Dr ND Luthuli, Operations Director at Auspicious Elites covered the health issues that young men need to be weary of especially STIs and HIV/Aids. Nolundi Luthuli, Managing Director at Auspicious Elites, then summed up the event by highlighting the important points.

Auspicious Elites would like to give a special thanks to the Manager of Community Development at uThungulu District Municipality, Mr P Mthethwa, for partnering with us in rolling out this programme. Our boys need to know that they are loved and that they have an important role to play in building our society. So we are very grateful to Mr Mthethwa for making this vision a reality. A special thanks to Foskor for seeing the value of the Boys2Men Leadership Talk and agreeing to support the event. We would also like to acknowledge Mr Thulani Maphosa for always being available to assist Auspicious Elites at a moment’s notice. And last but not least, to the Life Orientation teacher of uMdlamfe Secondary School Mrs T Le Roux for showing such appreciation for the work Auspicious Elites is doing for the youth.

Auspicious Elites Career Fair - August 2015

The Auspicious Elites Cair Fair is just one example of our educational programmes. The event took place at Hlanganani Hall in Esikhaleni Township and was supported by the Department of Education.

The aim of the event was to inform grade 9 learners about the various career options available after matric and to prepare them adequately as they choose subjects to pursue in grade 10. Teachers and learners were advised by the representatives of various companies and institutions of higher learning on what the requirements are for their respective jobs.

SiyaQubeksa Forestry gave a presentation on opportunities available in the Forestry Industry. The Department of Clinical Technology at Durban University of Technology advised learners on what subjects to take if they wish to be a Clinical Technologist. The media and television industry was represented by 1KZNtv and Urban Brew Studios. There were also presentations from the Department pf Environmental Affairs in KZN, University of Zululand was represented by the Department of Hydrology from Stats SA Sourcing funds for this year’s event proved to be a real challenge. But through the support of the uMhlathuze Municipality’s Ward 19 Councillor and the Youth Council, we were able to pull through. ABI has supplied refreshments for the event since 2013 when the project was launched.

In 2015 they once again came to the party, supplying cooldrinks for the event. It was an honour to receive sponsorship from SiyaQubeka Forestry in the form of meals for exhibitors and T-shirts for the learners. GX Marketing provided the printing of the programmes which were beautifully designed by Mr T Maphosa.Auspicious Elites would like to thank all those who believe in our vision and support the work we do. We would like to urge more companies to come on board and support us in future. Shaping future leaders is our mission and without the support of business it would be impossible to achieve this goal.

Auspicious Elites Leadership Talk and Motivational Talk - July 2015

On 23 July 2015 Auspicious Elites was invited to uMdlamfe High School at eSikhaleni to motivate Grade 11 learners and to host a Leadership Talk for Grade 12. The Motivational Talk was aimed at encouraging the Grade 11 class to work hard in preparation for grade 12 and giving them study skills to help them achieve their goals. Nolundi used her own life experience to motivate the learners to aim high. The Leadership Talk for grade 12 was sponsored by Boxer Stores Empangeni who provided the refreshments.

The main aim was to encourage a positive lifestyle focusing on sexual health and financial management. Absa Bank gave the learners tips on saving and also advised them on learnership opportunities and study loans.

School Visits and Motivation - February 2015

On the 2nd of February Auspicious Elites continued on its trail to hand over bursary forms from Transnet to deserving Grade 12 learners. This time we visited Birdswood Secondary in Richards’s bay. We met with the principal Mr Mazibuko and took photos with the pupils and Mr S Zwane.

On the 5th of February we visited Dlamvuzo High School where Deputy Principal, Mr Mzimela welcomed us and introduced us to the pupils. We had a brief chat explaining the purpose of our visit. The learners were highly motivated and promised to work harder. Managing Director Ms N Luthuli was invited to be guest Speaker at the Birdswood Secondary Orientation Day on the 7th of February. She gave a motivational talk inspiring teachers, pupils and parents to reach for their goals regardless of the circumstances they face.

Thursday, 12 February 2015 Auspicious Elites visited Richards Bay Secondary in Brackenham. We were accompanied by ABI who provided the sound system for the motivational talk. Nolundi Luthuli inspired the learners to work hard and to reach for the sky. The 5 top Grade 12 learners in maths and science received bursary forms from Transnet. The visit was very successful and the learners applauded Auspicious Elites’s MD, Nolundi Luthuli for her encouraging motivation.

We visited the last of our targeted schools, Khula High School on 19 February 2015 to hand over Transnet Bursary forms to deserving learners. We met with the principal Mr Nxumalo and he was very grateful for the support.

Premier Senzo Mchunu Visits Esikhaleni Schools - January 2015

On 21 January, we were fortunate to be part of the delegation that accompanied the Premier of KwaZulu/Natal Honourable Mr Semzo Mchunu as he visited schools in Esikhaleni. The Premier came to one of our adopted schools namely, uMdlamfe High School to inspect the school. He consulted with the Principal, Mr Mkhize and observed the learning conditions of the pupils in general. Auspicious Elites’s Managing Director, Nolundi Luthuli witnessed the visit as she formed part of the delegation which included concerned members of the community.

Auspicious Elites had a meeting with Circuit Manager in the Umhlathuze region to request permission for the activities to be conducted in 2015 under the banner of Auspicious Elites. We also took the opportunity to hand over the Transnet Bursary Forms to be distributed to schools in the whole region.

On 22 January Mondi SiyaQhubeka Forestry donated 10 Boxes (R190ea) of printing paper to Auspicious Elites. Ms Nonkululeko Nsibande coordinated the donation and Nolundi Luthuli received the donation on behalf of Auspicious Elites.

We are very grateful to SiyaQhubeka Forestry for their generous donation. Ms Nsibande also offered a donation to the value of R10 000 toward this year’s Auspicious Elites Career Fair.Auspicious Elites visited tow schools to deliver Transnet Bursary forms to the top 5 learners in each school. We visited Mdlamfe High School and Hlamvana Secondary School.
